Quincy Jones, Jr.

Biography

Quincy Delight Jones Jr., born on March 14, 1933, has played a vital role in the music industry since 1952 by working as a record producer and executive for numerous celebrities. The songwriter for a variety of genres has earned accolades including a Grammy Legend Award in 1992 and a Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Induction. First introduced to music through religious singing with his mother and neighbors around age 5 or 6, Quincy Jones began traveling with a band by the age 20 during a tour that he has personally acknowledged for influencing his perspective on racism in America.

Jones reached a breakthrough in 1961 after being promoted to the Vice President of Mercury Records leading to a multitude of successes during the early and mid-sixties. After receiving support from the honorable Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr, Jones collaborated to originate the charitable organization named Institute for Black American Music (IBAM), a charity that generates enough compensation to afford the funding of the National Library of African American Music and Art. Quincy Jones has created and collaborated with many charities and events for many years and his legacy of music and philanthropy continues to influence people across the world.

Music

Quincy Jones has created careers, produced classics, and collaborated with musical legends in the industry. In 1958, he first worked with Frank Sinatra leading to his employment as conductor for Sinatra when he recorded his entire second album just six years later. Jones offered to produce a record for Michael Jackson while working with on the musical theatrical feature titled The Wiz, resulting in the consecutive productions of Off the Wall and Thriller albums (respectively) solidifying Quincy as the most power producer in the industry at the time.

The influence of Quincy Jones even extends to other genres as well, such as the Brazilian music culture where he worked with major international music artists like: Simone, Ivan Lins, and Milton Nascimento. Jones truly exhibited his influence after the 1985 American Music Awards ceremony when he united most of the major recording artists in the music industry at the time to record "We Are the World", a song that raised money for the victims of famine in Ethiopia. Quincy Jones has created and collaborated with many charities and events for many years and his legacy of music and philanthropy continues to influence people across the world.

Film

With a footprint on the music industry that is only rivaled by his chokehold on major production scores in film, Jones helms an overwhelming discography of big motion picture scores beginning in 1964 after composing the music for the movie, The Pawnbroker. The tune titled Soul Bossa Nova from the Big Band Bossa Nova Album that Jones produced in 1962 was chosen to be the theme song for the 1997 spy comedy movie, Austin Power - International Man of Mystery, the first installment of several in a series for the franchise. After founding Qwest Productions in 1975, he worked with bot Michael Jackson and Diana Ross to produce the soundtrack for The Wiz, a majority African American casted musical adaptation of the popular film The Wizard of Oz.

In 1985, The Color Purple marked the debut of Quincy as a film producer after earning 10 Oscars for various fields in film and an additional Oscar for the musical score that Jones produced. The Fresh Prince of Bel Air was a critically-acclaimed television show produced by Quincy Jones that introduced the world to Will Smith debuting as a television sitcom actor in 1990. The book titled Q - The Autobiography of Quincy Jones was autobiography written by Quincy Jones that was published during 2001.
